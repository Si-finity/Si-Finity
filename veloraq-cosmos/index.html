Planets of Our Solar System

The solar system consists of eight planets orbiting the Sun, each with unique physical characteristics and atmospheric conditions. Studying planets helps scientists understand planetary formation and evolution.

From rocky terrestrial planets like Earth and Mars to gas giants such as Jupiter and Saturn, every planet contributes to our understanding of cosmic mechanics and gravitational interactions.

Exoplanets and Habitable Worlds

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars beyond our solar system. Thousands of such worlds have been discovered using modern space telescopes and detection methods like the transit technique.

Some exoplanets exist in habitable zones where liquid water may be possible. Although these worlds are distant, their study expands our understanding of planetary diversity and the potential for extraterrestrial life.
